UX Case Study

FIFA 20 Global Series

Rethinking competitiveness in one of the biggest sport video games in the world — redesigning the fan-facing experience and increasing player feedback by 32x

Product Designer · 2019–2020 Esports · B2C · Fan Experience Adobe XD · Miro · Qualtrics

01

The Project

What Is the FIFA 20 Global Series

The FIFA 20 Global Series was EA's competitive esports program for FIFA — the largest competitive football video game in the world. It brought together professional and amateur players across multiple regions, competing in tournaments with prize pools, live broadcasts, and a global audience of millions. The fan-facing website was the central hub where players discovered events, watched live matches, tracked results, and engaged with the competitive scene.

My Role

I was the Product Designer responsible for the full redesign of the FIFA 20 Global Series fan-facing website. The project spanned the complete design process — from prior research and experience assessment through information architecture, wireframing, and high-fidelity mockups. I worked closely with the product and engineering teams at Battlefy, who powered the competitive infrastructure behind EA's esports programs.

Two Audiences

Players — competitive FIFA players who needed to discover tournaments, register, track schedules, and follow results across their region. They wanted a personalized experience that surfaced relevant events and rewarded engagement.

Viewers — fans who watched live broadcasts, followed their favorite competitors, and consumed highlight content. They needed easy access to live streams, match stats, and video recaps.

The redesigned FIFA 20 Global Series home page — desktop and mobile versions featuring live match integration, video content, and event discovery.

02

The Problem

A Competitive Experience That Wasn't Competing

Data showed that the competitive experience for EA FIFA 20 was not reflecting the current needs of its consumers: players and viewers. The existing website had significant usability issues that were hurting engagement across the board. A full redesign was proposed with one core question: how might we increase engagement with viewership and rewards in mind?

What the Assessment Revealed

Navigation needed reprioritization. The internal navigation buried important sections like official rules and about pages under equal weight, when they didn't need that much attention. Meanwhile, CTAs for account creation and login weren't visible enough — the rewards for watching content weren't being communicated convincingly.

The main content area wasn't personalized. The primary real estate should have been curated to the logged-in user or shown more relevant and dynamic content like live broadcasts or favorite team content. Instead, it showed generic, static information.

Event content lacked diversity. The event section could have been improved by showing more diverse content. The logos looked alike and the content was not curated to a specific region or favorite team — it was showing irrelevant events to most visitors.

Carousel components hid content. Carousel components could be replaced by a grid system. Hiding content was not an ideal experience — a grid with "Load more" buttons could be more effective at surfacing relevant content.

03

Proposed Information Architecture

Auditing the Current State

The first step was a thorough experience assessment — mapping the current website side by side with a proposed IA that addressed every issue identified. Each section of the existing page was annotated with specific problems and opportunities, then mapped to a new structure with clear rationale for every architectural decision.

Key IA Decisions

Internal navigation. Prioritize CTAs for viewership. Work on current IA to add and remove items based on importance to the competitive experience.

Live events. Show what's currently happening on FGS. Prioritize favorite team results and region. Include live scoring so viewers never miss a moment.

Favorite team & rewards. Motivate users to watch content for coins (rewards system). Enable users to view events pages and brackets for more info on each event.

Personal adjustments. Region selector (auto set from account), favorite team selection, and My Coins rewards system for content consumption — making the experience feel tailored to each visitor.

Video content. Show broadcasts or live events. Content could be a game, recap, or news. Include CTA for coins rewards to incentivize viewership.

Recent articles & news. Most recent news globally related to events and league updates. Ordered based on region, showing global news or updates that impact the whole experience.

04

Wireframes

Exploring the Structure

With the IA validated, I moved into wireframing to explore layout options and content hierarchy. The wireframes focused on two key scenarios: the standard home experience and the live broadcast state where matches were actively being played. Both desktop and mobile needed to handle these states gracefully.

Broadcast Live States

A critical addition was designing for the live experience. When a match was in progress, the home page needed to transform — surfacing the live stream, match scores, and real-time stats without disrupting the overall navigation flow. The "Picture-in-Picture" variant allowed viewers to keep watching while browsing other content.

Wireframes for the home page across three states: default, broadcast live, and Picture-in-Picture — all designed for both desktop and mobile.

05

Navigation Components

Global & Internal Navigation

The navigation system was split into two layers. The global navigation served as the EA-wide header — consistent across all EA products with login, help center, and home links. The internal navigation was specific to the FIFA 20 Global Series, reprioritized to surface Match, Events, Rewards, Standings, and News as the primary paths.

On mobile, the internal navigation collapsed into an expandable menu that maintained the same priority hierarchy while saving screen real estate for content.

Navigation components: global EA header and FIFA 20 Global Series internal navigation — desktop and mobile states including the expanded mobile menu.

06

High-Fidelity Mockups — Desktop

Bringing the Redesign to Life

The high-fidelity mockups brought together all the research, IA decisions, and wireframe explorations into a polished, production-ready design. The home page featured a dark, immersive aesthetic that matched EA's competitive brand identity — with live match integration, video content grids, event discovery, and team personalization all working together.

Multiple States & Themes

The design system supported multiple home page states: the default experience with latest videos and news, the live match state with real-time scores and match facts, a Picture-in-Picture mode for continued browsing during live streams, and team selection for personalization. An alternate color theme was also explored to accommodate different tournament branding needs.

Desktop mockups: default home, live match, live match with stats, Picture-in-Picture browsing, team selection, and alternate tournament theme.

07

High-Fidelity Mockups — Mobile

Mobile-First Competitive Experience

The mobile experience was designed to give fans full access to the competitive scene from their phones. Every feature available on desktop — live matches, event details, video content, team selection — was carefully adapted for mobile with touch-friendly interactions and optimized content density.

The live match state on mobile prioritized the score and key match facts, with expandable sections for deeper stats. The team selection and alternate theme states ensured personalization worked seamlessly across devices.

Mobile mockups: default home, full page scroll, live match with stats, team selection, and alternate tournament theme — all optimized for touch interaction.

08

Player Ratings Feature

The Feedback Problem

Players could submit feedback on their tournament experience, but interactions were extremely low — only 0.5% of all players who participated in tournaments were leaving ratings. The initial goal was to grow this by 3%, but through iterative design and testing, the results far exceeded expectations.

Inspired by Uber

The approach was inspired by how Uber handles rating and feedback in their app. Uber's system succeeds because it appears at the right moment, requires minimal effort, and provides structured options for both positive and negative feedback. I adapted this model for tournament experiences — a thumbs up/down binary choice that expanded into contextual follow-up questions.

Three Iterations of Testing

This feature went through at least three iterations. User flows were created to define precisely when rating modals would appear — after login (floating card), on tournament pages, on match pages, and after tournament completion. Each iteration tested different triggers, copy variations, and feedback flows. After reaching the goal of increasing ratings on each iteration, the final round showed an increase of 3200% in player ratings.

The Rating Flow

The flow started with a simple question: "Enjoying this tournament?" with thumbs up or thumbs down. A positive response led to a "Give compliment" modal with options like "Well organized," "Clear rules," and "Great prizing." A negative response surfaced a "What can be improved?" modal with structured options like wait times, admin management, format issues, skill balance, and bugs. Both paths ended with a thank you confirmation and a CTA to follow the organizer or discover new tournaments.

Different copy was used for ongoing versus finished tournaments — "Enjoying this tournament?" for active events, and "Did you enjoy this tournament?" for completed ones.

Organizer Dashboard

The collected feedback fed into an Organizer Dashboard — giving tournament organizers visibility into their player count, new followers, approval ratings over time, and detailed breakdowns of compliments and improvements. This created a feedback loop that incentivized organizers to improve their tournament quality based on real player data.

09

Results & Impact

The FIFA 20 Global Series redesign and Player Ratings feature delivered measurable improvements to the competitive experience for both players and organizers.

32x Player Feedback Increase

Player ratings on tournaments went from 0.5% to 16% participation — a 3200% increase through iterative UX improvements to the feedback flow.

3+ Design Iterations

At least three full iterations of user flows and testing were conducted, each one surpassing the previous engagement target.

Full IA Redesign Delivered

Complete information architecture overhaul with personalization, live match integration, and rewards-driven engagement across desktop and mobile.

Feedback Loop Organizer Insights

A new Organizer Dashboard gave tournament hosts real-time visibility into player satisfaction, compliments, and areas for improvement.

The Bigger Picture

This project demonstrated that a structured design process — from research through iterative testing — could transform engagement metrics in competitive gaming. The IA redesign made the fan-facing experience relevant and personalized, while the player ratings feature created a virtuous cycle between player feedback and organizer improvement. Both projects became foundational patterns that could be applied across other EA esports titles on the Battlefy platform.
